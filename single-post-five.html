Don’t Despise Your Small Beginnings!

Author: Fayiah S. Tamba

The Stories of great and successful people are often and widely read with a little lesson learned from how they transitioned from not enough to more than enough. Almost all the celebrants we admired today must have had a small beginnings. They probably didn’t reach the top overnight. Great opportunities surrounded them daily from the little things they were committed to doing.

The road to success is tough for those who want to get there in a shortest possible time; but easy for people who are ready and willing to go through the required processes. Make sure to use wisely every opportunity that is available to you. Managing your small beginning predicts how you will manage a bigger ones. All your wishes in life may not be fulfilled at once until you tackle them bit by bit.

Take pleasure in the little things you are doing now. They are the bridges that will cross you to the other side of greatness. Sometimes nobody recognizes that little things you are doing but never give-up. God sees your determinations and at the appropriate time, He will use that little thing to make people to recognize and reward you. No condition is permanent! Whatever seems little that you are doing today, is the beginning of your greatness for tomorrow. Don't despise your small beginnings.

The way up is the way down. As you climb the ladder to greatness, know that little is much when God is in it. It’s not how long you’ve been doing the same thing but how well. Or else you won't get a different result. Life is such that we have to experience small things before we see big things. Whether you are starting a new job that cannot fully sustain you or selling a type of product that you are ashamed of and thinking about quitting, I suggest you hold on in those areas until great opportunities avail themselves.

Whatever little things you are doing now, be at your best so that you outweigh your competitors. Some years ago, many Liberian women and girls were ashamed to sell on the streets. They depended on men for everything. But today, things have changed. I see many potential and beautiful girls selling cold water, sugar cane, cookies, and plastic bags on the streets of Monrovia and this is just the beginnings of their greatness.

Few years ago, I didn’t despise my small beginnings. I sold on the streets, between cars, in video clubs, and other places. I was not ashamed doing that because I had to assist my parents with provision in the home. I sold roasted cassavas, peanuts, mangos, candies, bananas, fuels and kerosenes, breads, face towels and other petit trades.

At the time of my small beginning, many people laughed at me and called me all sort of names because I was carrying waiter on my head and holding bucket in my hand. I didn't care about their mockeries. I was focused and determinated to help alleviate the poverty from my family and I. Howbeit, I understand that the Liberian slogan that says “small, small” reinforces our courage and commitment to push forward in life regardless of whatever results we see now. We should focus on doing the small things now and expect big things later.
